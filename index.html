As with all servers, your local administrators can block or suspend your account, and they can delete your posts. They can also block individual users from other servers from appearing on yours or block entire servers.

Servers can independently block your account from appearing on their server, or block your entire server. The more accounts following you on a specific server, the more control that server's administrators and moderators have over whether you can reach their users.

Your ability to see posts from the accounts you follow is also dependent on the moderation of the individual servers that they are on. As with your followers, servers can independently block your account from fetching posts or block your entire server. In addition, the accounts you follow may be suspended by the administrators of their servers, or have their posts deleted by their server's moderators. As with followers, the more accounts you follow on a server, the more control that server's administrators and moderators have over what you see.

How Does This Work?

This tool works by querying the public Mastodon APIs on the account's server; thus, at this time, it is only expected to work with Mastodon instances (sorry). It does so without authenticating, so it can only see information made public by the server admins and/or account holders. Some servers do not publicly report information such as activity, and some accounts have their following and followers relationships hidden.

Privacy

All work is done entirely in your browser; no information is sent to the operator of this website. The only server contacted is the one that hosts the account.
