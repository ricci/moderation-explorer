Who Moderates My Posts and Timeline?

Your Mastodon account is subject to moderation on the instance where it resides. Similarly, those you follow and those who follow you are subject to the moderation on their own instances. This tool helps you understand which moderators control what you say and see on the Fediverse.

Blocked Servers

As with all servers, your local administrators can block or suspend your account, and they can delete your posts. They can also block individual users from other instances from appearing on yours or block entire instances.

The amount of work your administrators and moderators have to do depends largely on the number of active users on your server and how much they post. Moderation reports regarding your posts can also come in from any of the peer servers across the fediverse.

Whether or not the accounts that follow you actually see your posts is dependent on the moderation of the individual servers that they are on. Servers can independently block your account from appearing on their instance, or block your entire instance. The more accounts following you on a specific server, the more control that server's administrators and moderators have over whether you can reach their users.
